Chapter 33: Chapter 33: Wherever He Goes, It Is His Territory
He connected this 20-meter Copper Pipe to the one already connected to the Ghost Fire, running it through the base of the City Wall.

It extended all the way outside the camp.

Sticking out for a length of one meter.

The next moment—

Once he finished the construction, the Copper Pipe solidified, and the cold, white flame from the Ghost Fire flowed into it.

And then he witnessed an incredibly shocking sight.

The area around the one-meter section of Copper Pipe extending outside the camp had actually become a safe zone!

"..."

Chen Fan’s breath hitched. He stared at the "Copper Pipe" building module on his Lord panel, and after a long moment, he couldn’t help but burst into laughter. "Good, good, this is great!"

This "Copper Pipe" could actually extend the range of the safe zone!

The Ghost Fire would flow along the Copper Pipe, and wherever it went, that would be his territory!

In that instant, his mind was flooded with wild architectural ideas!

For example...

Night roads!

Traveling at night was impossible in this world, but with the Copper Pipes, he could build a road that was passable during the eternal night.

When he had enough resources in the future, he could even encircle the entire continent with Copper Pipes. The whole Eternal Night Continent, as far as the eye could see, would be his domain!

If the Eternal Night Continent were a body,

then his Copper Pipes would be the capillaries spreading throughout it.

And this was just one extremely basic use for the "Copper Pipe".

...

He squatted outside the camp, looking at the "Copper Pipe" lying on the cracked Wilderness. With a thought, the pipe once again turned into a "wireframe" composed of lines, and a "two-pipe, three-way connector" appeared at its end.

It could connect three Copper Pipes.

This building module had a huge advantage: even after solidifying, it could return to its wireframe state to be freely modified without consuming any resources.

Right now, this Copper Pipe was 10 centimeters wide.

The safe zone around this Copper Pipe was half a meter on each side, for a total of one meter.

He wanted to see if adding a thicker pipe would change the range of the safe zone.

He immediately built another 1-meter-wide Copper Pipe and connected it to one of the ports. The other two ports remained sealed as they weren’t connected to anything.

He was, in fact, experimenting.

After all, connecting a 10-centimeter pipe to a 1-meter pipe should be somewhat difficult.

Talk about a mismatch.

The sizes were completely different.

But—

When in doubt, leave it to the panel.

As the two pipe wireframes slowly merged, the pipes of two completely different sizes fused together seamlessly, with no trace of a weld, as if they were made that way from the start.

At the same time.

The safe zone on both sides of this one-meter "Copper Pipe" was still half a meter each, for a total of one meter.

But including the footprint of the "Copper Pipe" itself, this section of safe zone protruding from the camp could be up to 2 meters wide.

"Phew..."

Chen Fan let out a light breath and stood up. With a thought, the Copper Pipe sank into the ground, burying itself beneath the desolate earth, leaving no trace.

Even Lame Monkey and the others had no idea what was happening. They only knew he seemed to be building something they had never seen before.

After all, Lame Monkey and his men could only see the actual range of the camp’s safe zone after the eternal night fell and the Ghost Fire’s halo of light spread out.

He was the master of this camp.

Naturally, he would be the first to know of any changes to it.

"Good stuff!"

Chen Fan grinned again. If an ordinary building was the number 1, with no freedom of choice—for example, if an Arrow Tower was over 3 meters tall, then it could only be built to that height, no shorter or taller—

It was fixed.

Then this building module was a 0.1, completely open to DIY.

Its width, length, connectors, whether it was straight, curved, or serpentine—all of it could be freely customized without consuming extra Ghost Stones. On its own, the building module was worthless.

But the building module could be applied to all buildings.

This meant—

He could use it to create a multitude of "1.8s," "2.7s," and "3.1s"—buildings with extreme freedom that perfectly suited his camp. His control and flexibility over the entire camp would be greater, giving him far more strategic options!

...

Chen Fan ignored the Copper Pipe for now. It was just an experimental piece. He needed to go test if the Copper Pipes had any synergies with other buildings.

For the next half-hour,

he was extremely busy.

He continuously connected various buildings with Copper Pipes, searching for any possible synergies. After using up 47 Ghost Stones to build pipes for all sorts of experiments, he finally stood on the City Wall, looking down at the camp within, a satisfied grin spreading across his face.

With his hands on his hips, his eyes were filled with pride.

This was his domain.

The once-chilly rainwater now looked like champagne at a victory celebration.

First, he connected the "two Arrow Towers" to the "Ghost Fire" using Copper Pipes.

Activating an Arrow Tower consumed Ghost Stones, but reloading them one by one was too troublesome. This was especially true when activating the Active Skill "Arrow Rain Storm," which required a reload after each use. Even if the reload time was extremely short, it was still a limitation.

What’s more,

as the number of Arrow Towers increased in the future, he wouldn’t be able to keep up.

But after connecting them,

the consumption of all Arrow Towers would prioritize the Ghost Stones within the "Ghost Fire," saving him the trouble of reloading them one by one. And if a special situation required using the "Arrow Rain Storm" skill multiple times in a row, there would be no delay.

It went from single-shot loading to fully automatic.

When an Arrow Tower activated, the Ghost Stones would shatter inside the "Ghost Fire," and their energy would be transmitted to the tower through the Copper Pipe.

While he was at it...

He also built a Copper Pipe with one end connected to the boiler on the stove inside the Wooden House and the other end leading to the roof. Rainwater would flow down the pipe into the boiler to be heated until boiling.

He built another Copper Pipe.

One end connected to the boiler, the other to the inside of the Wooden House’s surrounding walls and beneath the floor.

The boiling water would flow into the pipes, keeping the house warm.

He named it the "Heating System 1.0".

He had gone inside to check it a moment ago.

The inside was extremely warm.

The damp, cold feeling from last night was completely gone.

Although the "Heating System 1.0" was nearly perfect, it had one small flaw: no pressure source. The hot water struggled to flow through the winding, undulating Copper Pipes. Without proper flow, the temperature wouldn’t hold for long, and this world didn’t have anything like a circulation pump.

But where there’s a will, there’s a way.

He already had a plan.

Known Fact 1: Earth Spike traps are triggered by weight.

Known Fact 2: Triggering an Earth Spike trap does not consume Ghost Stones.

Conclusion:

This was a multi-triggerable, zero-resource... "Power Source," or what could be understood as a "Piston."

It would only wear out and shatter after a very long period of use.

So, he planned to use this Piston to build a heating system that could automatically circulate hot water.

However, that was his planned "Heating System 2.0."

Tonight, he had to focus on other things. For now, the hot water in "Heating System 1.0" had to be replaced manually. When the temperature dropped, he had to disconnect the Copper Pipe, drain it, and refill it.

It was relatively troublesome.

But it was good enough for now.

...

The building module offered extremely high freedom, but it couldn’t be upgraded; there was no upgrade option.

"Now *this* is what it means to be an Architect..."

Standing on the City Wall, Chen Fan looked down at the camp, his eyes filled with satisfaction. The original camp had several buildings, but he’d always felt there was a certain rigidity to it. But now, with the addition of the "Copper Pipes"...

...they were like blood vessels, connecting all the buildings.

Now, he finally felt that the entire camp had been truly activated.

He finally felt like it was an extension of his own body.

...

After finishing all this, Chen Fan cheerfully walked down from the City Wall, preparing to head to the damaged section that had been attacked by the Meat Worm Ghost last night.

The Meat Worm Ghost’s self-destruction had indeed caused a certain amount of damage to that section of the City Wall.

However, it wasn’t serious and hadn’t affected the foundation.

Just then—

Zhou Mo and his subordinate appeared outside the camp, walking through the downpour. Looking at the people inside, he called out loudly, "Station Chief, we’re back."

Lame Monkey quickly went forward. After testing them to confirm they weren’t impostors, he brought the two to Chen Fan.

"Station Chief."

"We were a step too late. Everyone at the Hill and Valley Royal Family’s Station 16 hanged themselves last night."

"Their bodies were already cold when we arrived."

"In the entire Wilderness... we’re the only station left..."

Not to mention the psychological pressure of having to rush back to the camp before dark.

"A pity."

Chen Fan smiled and took the pouch.

He naturally understood the meaning behind Zhou Mo’s words.

"Go get some rest."

There was still one hour until nightfall.

Time was tight.

Chen Fan walked alone to the section of the City Wall that had been damaged by the Meat Worm Ghosts the previous night. The viscous green liquid left behind by their explosions had corroded parts of the wall, leaving many fist-sized holes.

The wall was three meters thick.

This level of damage had almost no effect on the City Wall, all things considered.

After all, these were just a few that slipped through, and their numbers weren’t too high.

The next moment—

The camp’s City Wall suddenly dissolved into a spectral blueprint composed of white lines of light. In the section before him, he could clearly see that several of the white lines were broken and needed repair.

-

[This repair requires 1 Ghost Stone.]

-

’The price isn’t too steep.’

Chen Fan took a Ghost Stone from his clothes and crushed it. The stone turned into a milky-white liquid that slowly flowed into the spectral blueprint. The broken white lines began to rapidly mend, a change visible to the naked eye.

A few moments later, the spectral blueprint solidified once more.

The City Wall before him was restored, completely intact and as good as new.

’I wonder how other Architects repair their City Walls.’

Chen Fan murmured to himself as he glanced at the darkening sky. He could repair the City Wall so easily because he had his panel. ’How do other Architects, the ones without panels, manage it?’

He knew that Jiangbei City was a natural Ghost Fire Zone of considerable size. The three major factions within it had joined forces to build a wall enclosing the entire city, a structure on a much grander scale than his own.

Without a way to repair their wall, the maintenance costs would be astronomical.

He didn’t dwell on it and began to count his Ghost Stones.

He had 508 in total.

It was a considerable sum, but it definitely wasn’t enough to upgrade the City Wall to Level 2, which required 1,600 Ghost Stones. However, it was enough to significantly bolster the camp’s defenses again.

The City Wall currently had two Level 3 Arrow Towers.

Their power was sufficient, but there weren’t enough of them.

Building a Level 1 Arrow Tower required 10 Ghost Stones.

He first spent 100 Ghost Stones to build ten Level 1 Arrow Towers, slotting them all neatly into the sockets behind the City Wall’s crenelations.

These ten Arrow Towers were made entirely of wood and looked rather fragile.

Their firepower was also lacking.

He then spent another 200 Ghost Stones to upgrade all ten Arrow Towers to Level 2.

As the Ghost Stones turned into milky-white liquid, the Arrow Towers began to transform. They seemed to grow as if alive, morphing into a mixed wood-and-stone structure. The towers themselves became significantly sturdier, and from the Upgrade Directions that popped up, he chose [Enhanced Crossbow Power] for all of them.

He then spent another 26 Ghost Stones to forge a 26-meter-long Copper Pipe, connecting these ten Arrow Towers to the Ghost Fire at the camp’s center.

He had already connected the two existing Level 3 Arrow Towers to the camp’s Ghost Fire.

Therefore, he only needed to extend the Copper Pipe from the original two towers, running it along the City Wall to connect to the ten new ones in sequence.

This was the shortest distance and the most resource-efficient route.

The next moment!

The Copper Pipes connected beneath the bases of the ten Arrow Towers on the City Wall glowed with a white light before quickly sinking and disappearing into the wall itself.

He placed 50 Ghost Stones into the Ghost Fire as a reserve.

This would cover the Ghost Fire’s daily consumption and provide energy for the Arrow Towers.

And with that—

the entire camp’s defenses had undergone a qualitative improvement.

...

"..."

Standing on the City Wall, Chen Fan looked at the rows of Arrow Towers and smiled in satisfaction. The camp’s security had been drastically improved once again.

He then gazed toward the distant horizon and murmured softly,

"Man can only do his part. I’ve done all I can."

"The rest is up to fate."

Although the camp’s security was greatly improved, it still wouldn’t be able to withstand a direct assault from a Meat Worms’ Ghost Tide.

After all this work, he had 131 Ghost Stones remaining.

He looked at a building on his Lord panel that he had yet to construct. He now had more than enough Ghost Stones to build it.

[Sacrificial Tower].

[Obtain special resources. Cost to build: 20 Ghost Stones.]

After 20 Ghost Stones shattered,

a spectral blueprint of the Sacrificial Tower appeared before him. It was only about a meter tall—an exquisite and compact seven-story pagoda.

It was made entirely of bluestone, with its eaves accented in a deep, inky red.

He placed it next to the Wooden House.

The circular City Wall enclosing the camp had a diameter of ten meters, so the interior space wasn’t very large. With a Wooden House, the Ghost Fire, and a Sentry Tower already in place, the remaining area was getting cramped.

But there was no helping it. Any larger, and the cost of the City Wall would have skyrocketed.

’This will have to do for now,’ he thought. ’I can make adjustments later on.’

Chen Fan looked at the compact Sacrificial Tower, and a panel slowly materialized before him.

-

[Sacrifice Ghost Corpses to obtain special resources.]

[The stronger and more numerous the sacrificed Ghost Corpses, the more valuable the resources obtained.]

[This building cannot be upgraded.]

-

’So that’s what the "Sacrificial" part means...’

Chen Fan nodded thoughtfully. ’This Building Effect is pretty good.’ The Ghost Corpses from the last few days had all been buried in the Farmland. While they served as fertilizer, the supply was becoming excessive.

The Sacrificial Tower provided an excellent way to dispose of future Ghost Corpses.

With nothing left to do, he quietly waited for the eternal night to fall.

...

「One hour later.」

A familiar, heavy darkness appeared on the horizon, enveloping the entire world like a tide.

The eggshell-like halo of light once again enveloped the camp.

"Huh?"

Lame Monkey, perched behind the crenelations, was the first to notice something was off. The camp’s normally circular safe zone now had a sudden bulge extending outward. A look of confusion crossed his face. "What the..."

"What do you think?"

Chen Fan chuckled, his gaze fixed on the gray fog beyond the camp as he patiently waited for the night’s Ghost Objects to arrive.

It was his latest masterpiece: a safe-zone overflow.

This inconspicuous bulge would be the starting point for many of his wild architectural plans in the future.

"...Why’d it grow a dick-tip?"

"..."

Chen Fan shot Lame Monkey a look before turning away, his expression flat. "Stay alert."

Chen Fan stood guard on the City Wall with Lame Monkey and a few others, preparing for the night’s crisis. Meanwhile, Zhou Mo and the others who had been busy all day were already asleep under his orders.

However...

...

Dawn had broken.

Chapter 36: Chapter 36: So Direct and Simple?
After waking up, Chen Fan washed up and stood at the entrance of the Wooden House, gazing out at the dreary, endless drizzle.

The rain wasn’t too heavy today.

The rainy seasons he was used to just meant more precipitation, but this world’s rainy season was a literal 24/7 affair. At best, it was a light drizzle like this; at worst, it was a torrential downpour.

"..."

He looked at the cracked earth outside.

Even after several days of rain, there was no sign of flooding; the ground remained parched and cracked. The narrow fissures, like the calloused soles of a farmer’s feet, were like bottomless monsters, swallowing all the rain that fell.

There wasn’t even any standing water.

In his memory,

the weather in this world was quite normal outside of the rainy season. A continuous downpour like this in the wilderness was clearly not normal. More importantly... when the rainy season arrived early, Ghost Objects tended to become unusually aggressive.

He didn’t dwell on it.

He looked at the two remaining Exotic Treasures in his hand. The Meat Worms’ Ghost Tide from the day before yesterday had left him three in total; the other one was the building module Blueprint for the "Copper Pipe."

The one in his hand was a small pocketknife, about the size of a keychain ornament.

-

[Exotic Treasure Name]: Big Saber.

[Exotic Treasure Level]: White.

[Exotic Treasure Effect]: Deals decent damage to Low Tier Ghost Objects.

-

"..."

Chen Fan frowned as he looked at the Exotic Treasure in his hand. This wasn’t the first Weapon Category Exotic Treasure he’d obtained; the Long Spear Lame Monkey always carried was one such item.

It was just that the name of Lame Monkey’s Exotic Treasure was extremely impressive: the "Godslaying Spear."

’How could this Exotic Treasure be...’

’Big Saber.’

’So direct and plain?’

’Besides, it has no special effects. The Godslaying Spear could even change its size at will.’

’Whatever.’

He set the Exotic Treasure aside, planning to give it to Zhou Mo when he returned. It was a Weapon Category Exotic Treasure, after all. It would still be more effective against Ghost Objects than a regular weapon.

As for the other Exotic Treasure,

it was rather special.

A small black lump wrapped in a membrane, it was a Seed-type Treasure.

-

[Exotic Treasure Name]: Meat Ghost Mother and Child Species.

[Exotic Treasure Level]: Green.

[Exotic Treasure Effect]: Must be planted in ’Level 2 Farmland.’ Matures in seven days. Upon ripening, it divides into a Mother-Child Fruit. If two people each consume one, they can communicate telepathically within a hundred li. The effect lasts for one month.

[Planting Conditions]: Must be watered with Ghost Blood every hour and fertilized with Ghost Corpses.

-

"Good stuff."

Chen Fan silently tucked the bundle of seeds into his clothes. This Exotic Treasure solved one of his biggest problems: the lack of communication methods. For instance, he currently had no way of contacting Zhou Mo, who was out on patrol.

The planting conditions were just a little troublesome.

It would require someone to be specifically assigned to watch over it at all times.

But that was fine.

He had his own professional farmers, the "Heng and Ha Generals," so it wasn’t a problem. The two of them could take turns watching it, a perfect solution.

This was the first Green Level Exotic Treasure he had obtained.

Its effect was also better than he had imagined.

A ripened Meat Ghost Mother-Child Fruit would likely be a hot commodity, even in the Jiangbei Marketplace.

It looked pretty good.

Otherwise, being drenched by rain all night during guard duty was something no one could endure. They weren’t made of iron, after all.

"Chief."

Chen Fan walked past the City Wall to the camp’s periphery. Qi Chong, who had been busy with something in the Farmland, immediately jogged over. "It’s all harvested."

"The 10 Monkey Head Mushroom seedlings grew a total of 10 Monkey Head Mushrooms."

"The 8 Ghost Stone Vein plants produced 80 Ghost Stones."

"I put them all in the Warehouse."

"Mm."

An earthy, fishy taste mixed with sweetness assaulted his mouth.

Meanwhile, many Bamboo Pole Ghosts had reached the base of the City Wall. They grabbed nearby Mantis Ghosts and, using their sharp Mantis Arms as battering rams, began striking the wall with all their might in an attempt to breach it.

In an instant, the entire camp was in peril, with enemies both inside and outside the walls.

They had been plunged into an extremely dangerous predicament.

...

"Every man for himself!"

After roaring the order, Chen Fan quickly scanned the situation outside the walls. The Meat Worm Ghosts were all gone. Though this section of the City Wall was corroded and battered, it hadn’t collapsed.

Numerous Bamboo Pole Ghosts were now focusing their assault on this weak point.

The Level 3 Arrow Tower, enhanced with the Fire Element, was now taking precise aim at the High-Level Bamboo Pole Ghost that had entered the camp. The power of a Level 3 Arrow Tower was nothing to scoff at; Crossbow Arrows rained down like hail.

One to the forehead.

Two to the eyes.

Three to the abdomen.

It stumbled forward only a few more steps before collapsing to the ground, utterly dead.

With their living catapult gone, no more Mantis Ghosts were thrown over the walls.

After taking out the High-Level Bamboo Pole Ghost, the tower turned its attention to the group of Ghosts at the base of the City Wall. It began picking them off one by one, and with support from five of the Level 2 Arrow Towers, they quickly cleared the Ghosts from the wall’s edge.

The other five Arrow Towers were busy killing the Mantis Ghosts that had landed inside the walls.

Meanwhile, the other Level 3 Arrow Tower, continuously activating its Arrow Rain Storm skill, was mowing down the large horde of Ghosts charging into the camp.

The whole camp seemed like a small boat in a tempest, about to be overwhelmed.

But in truth, it was rock solid.

Their job was simple: stay alive. They just had to avoid the few Mantis Ghosts that had made it into the camp and let the camp’s City Defense Buildings handle the rest!

Just then—a Mantis Ghost burst from the shadows. It leaped into the air, its twin blades raised, and screeched as it hacked down at Chen Fan and his men.

In a flash of lightning, Chen Fan could even see the fine hairs next to the corner of the Ghost’s mouth.

Ghastly and unsettling.

"CLANG!"

Lame Monkey, wielding the Godslaying Spear, was the first to react. He raised the spear and blocked the blow. The immense force of the impact slammed him to the ground, but he had successfully thwarted the Mantis Ghost’s surprise attack.

The next instant, a Crossbow Arrow sliced through the rain—a direct hit.

The Mantis Ghost, given no chance for a second attack, froze in place and collapsed onto the rain-slicked City Wall.

...

Mere moments later, the battle was over.

The ground outside the walls was littered with Ghost Corpses. One section of the wall looked ready to collapse, but inside the camp, not a single living Ghost remained.

"..."

Chen Fan quickly scanned his men, then looked back inside the walls.

No one was injured.

Zhou Mo, who had been sleeping, was now awake. He stood in the rainy night, panting, Big Saber in hand. Blood seeped from the web of his thumb where he’d gripped his weapon, and he was looking up at Chen Fan.

’He’d never fought a Mantis Ghost head-on before.’

’A single, well-aimed Crossbow Arrow from a Level 2 Arrow Tower could instantly kill a Mantis Ghost.’

’But... I just saw Zhou Mo barely hold his own after exchanging a few blows with a Mantis Ghost. Even then, the repeated impacts made his hand bleed. A Level 2 Cultivator, armed with an Exotic Treasure, couldn’t even defeat a cannon-fodder Mantis Ghost in a direct confrontation.’

’Ghost Objects... they aren’t something humans can fight head-on.’

’It’s difficult even for Cultivators.’

’And this is inside the camp, where there’s light.’

’Out in the Eternal Night, surrounded by thick darkness with zero visibility, Zhou Mo wouldn’t have survived three moves against its blades.’

Chen Fan’s gaze shifted to the sedan chair in the gray fog outside the camp.

At this point, all the Ghost Objects that had surrounded the camp tonight had been wiped out. Only the sedan chair remained.

’Is it going to retreat?’

Chen Fan frowned as he watched the blood-red sedan chair slowly retreating into the gray fog. ’This female ghost is too intelligent. She’s big trouble. If I let her get away this time, she’ll definitely come back with even more Ghosts.’

’But there isn’t much I can do.’

’I can only watch it leave. The Arrow Towers can only attack targets within the camp’s perimeter.’

The next moment—the four Bamboo Pole Ghosts carrying the sedan chair, after retreating several meters, suddenly turned and charged toward the camp. They picked up speed, rushing straight into the camp with the sedan chair on their shoulders. The instant they crossed the threshold...

The sedan chair exploded violently,

shattering into countless shards that embedded themselves in the ground.

The four Bamboo Pole Ghosts carrying it were also blown to pieces.

"AHAHAHA!!!"

A piercing, unhinged laugh instantly enveloped the entire camp. A completely naked female ghost stood barefoot on the ground, her gaze fixed on the figures on the City Wall.

Her cheeks were as pale as snow, not the face of a living person.

Her features were exquisite, like a masterpiece carved by a master artisan.

Her eyes held no pupils, only a chaotic, inky blackness, yet her lips were an exceptionally vivid red.

Her jet-black hair cascaded past her waist, ending at her calves.

’So beautiful.’

That was Chen Fan’s first impression. She was so beautiful she didn’t seem like a Ghost Object at all, but more like an otherworldly enchantress.

’A completely nude woman doesn’t actually possess much aesthetic beauty; it’s often the suggestion of clothing, the half-hidden form, that truly accentuates it.’

’But—’

’—this female ghost before me is utterly without clothing, laid bare to the world.’

’Yet there isn’t a hint of vulgarity about her. She exudes an aura of noble, icy detachment.’
