There were currently five Beast Traps in the camp. He had earned quite a few Ghost Stones with them over the past few days, and it only cost two Ghost Stones to build one. 
Upgrading a Beast Trap to Level 2 wasn't expensive either, requiring only ten Ghost Stones. 
He decided to see what it could do. 
The next moment. 
The ten Ghost Stones on the ground instantly shattered, turning into a milky-white liquid that flowed into the Beast Trap beside the Farmland. A panel slowly materialized before him. 
- 
「Beast Trap upgraded to Level 2, becoming 'Earth Spike'. Must be placed in a fixed location, cannot be moved at will, can be triggered multiple times.」 
「Please select an Upgrade Direction.」 
「1: Blends in with the surrounding environment, making it extremely concealed and difficult to detect.」 
「2: Increased power.」 
「3: Increased Earth Spike retraction speed.」 
- 
The original Beast Trap had transformed into the phantom image of a square Earth Spike structure, one meter long and one meter wide, awaiting his placement. 
He chose the second Upgrade Direction. 
He then placed it directly in front of the Farmland. 
It looked rather conspicuous. 
On the cracked, desolate ground, there was a square patch of empty land dotted with dozens of neat holes, looking like the heart of a tree bored through by woodworms. He picked up a Ghost Corpse nearby—one that Lame Monkey hadn't buried in the Farmland yet—and tossed it on top of the holes. 
The next moment! 
"CLICK!" 
With a soft sound, dozens of thick, hard, black Wooden Spikes shot out from the ground like striking vipers, moving at a speed almost imperceptible to the naked eye. They instantly pierced through the Ghost Corpse. 
Its power was on a completely different level than the Beast Trap. 
Only then did the hard Wooden Spikes begin to slowly retract. After a few breaths, they disappeared back into the holes. The next time something stepped on it, it would trigger again! 
This made up for a major flaw of the Beast Trap: its inability to be triggered repeatedly. 
Its power had also increased significantly. 
"Not bad." 
Chen Fan nodded with slight satisfaction. 'Cheap, effective, and it runs for free. It doesn't need Ghost Stones to power it. As long as it doesn't break, I can use it indefinitely.' 
If he had chosen the third Upgrade Direction, the Earth Spike's retraction speed would have been faster, allowing it to trigger more frequently. 
'But for a trap like the Earth Spike, the most important thing is the ability to kill in one hit.' He believed it was crucial to instantly eliminate any Ghost Object that stepped on it. 
'No matter how fast it is, it's useless if it's not powerful enough.' 
... 
He still had 46 Ghost Stones left. 
Standing by the Farmland, Chen Fan rose and looked toward the gradually darkening horizon, his eyes narrowing slightly. In his construction panel, there was a building called the "Sacrificial Tower," which required 20 Ghost Stones to build. 
Its effect was described rather vaguely. 
- 
「Level 1 Sacrificial Tower」: Can be used to obtain special resources. Cost to build: 20 Ghost Stones. 
- 
He definitely wasn't planning on holding onto too many Ghost Stones tonight. He was debating whether to build more Earth Spikes or to construct the Sacrificial Tower. The former would provide the greatest immediate boost to the camp's defenses. 
The latter, based on its description alone, was most likely related to the mid-to-late stages of the camp's development. The special resources it provided probably wouldn't be useful in the short term. Besides, those resources wouldn't be given to him for free; he'd probably have to trade Ghost Stones for them.
It was basically a Gold-devouring Beast of a building. 
After a long moment. 
He took out 40 Ghost Stones and upgraded all four remaining Beast Traps to Level 2. He chose the second Upgrade Direction for all of them and placed them in a neat row on the side of the Farmland closest to the camp's edge. 
The Farmland was five meters wide. 
One Earth Spike was one meter wide, so five Earth Spikes were five meters wide. 
They fit perfectly with the Farmland. 
The arrangement was remarkably neat and orderly, the kind of setup that would satisfy a perfectionist. 
"Much better." 
Chen Fan looked at his masterpiece with satisfaction. 'With these five Earth Spikes, my Farmland should be reasonably well-protected. The Arrow Towers on the City Wall can handle the rest of the Ghost Objects.' In the end, he decided to put the Sacrificial Tower out of his mind. 
'Survive tonight first.' 
'As long as I survive, I'll have plenty of time to figure out what the Sacrificial Tower is. If I don't make it through the night, it doesn't matter how amazing it is.' 
Besides, he felt that the Farmland was more important than the Arrow Towers right now. Once the Ghost Ore Vein Seed matured, he would receive a windfall of Ghost Stones. He just didn't know how many. Perhaps there would be a small surprise.

He looked up at the horizon. This was the most he could do. As long as tonight's Ghost Tide didn't kill him right away, he could use the Ghost Stones dropped by the Ghost Objects to strengthen his camp even more, raising its limits once again! 
It was an opportunity. 
And also a crisis. 
Everything depended on whether he could survive the night.

He couldn't help but puff out his chest. Raising the white-glowing item in his hand, he handed it to Chen Fan with a wide grin. "Lord, I got it." 
"You did well." 
On the rain-swept City Wall, Chen Fan took the Exotic Treasure from Xiaoyu's hand, his expression complicated. "Why did you run for the Farmland just now, instead of back to the City Wall?" 
"There wasn't enough time." 
Xiaoyu's throat felt raw, perhaps from shouting so loudly earlier. Now that the adrenaline was fading, his legs felt weak and rubbery. "The Ghost Object was faster than me and it was closer. If I had run for the City Wall, I never would have made it." 
"Even if it didn't catch me, the City Wall would have been breached for sure." 
"Brother Wang has been good to me, and you, Lord, took me in during this rainy season. Everyone is struggling to survive. I didn't want to be the one to get everyone killed. If someone had to die, it was better that it was just me." 
"Besides, I figured with the Arrow Towers, the number of Ghost Objects had to be dwindling. If I could just make it to the traps and hold out for a little while, maybe we could survive this crisis." 
"..." 
Chen Fan didn't say much more. He simply patted Xiaoyu on the shoulder. "I remember, you're Xiaoyu, right? That was a decisive call. You did an excellent job." 
In reality, the scenario Xiaoyu described—the wall being breached and everyone dying—wouldn't have happened. 
If time had run out, he would have closed the gate. 
But... 
He just hadn't expected Xiaoyu to handle it so perfectly. It was a decisive call, but more importantly, it showed a spirit of self-sacrifice. Honestly, very few people in that situation would have chosen not to run for the City Wall, all because they were worried about endangering everyone else. 
'I definitely couldn't have done that.' 
He could keenly sense that Xiaoyu's actions had stirred something new in everyone. It was a visible sign that they had been emotionally moved. If they faced another crisis, perhaps others would now make the same choice. 
Humans are social creatures. 
Emotions are contagious. 
For now, no more Ghost Objects were charging the camp. He narrowed his eyes, peering into the dark, rainy night. 'What just happened probably wasn't a coincidence,' he thought. 'Could it really be such a fluke that the Ghost Objects in the darkness attacked the very moment Xiaoyu left the wall and reached the edge of the camp?' 
And... 
This wave of attacking Ghost Objects, which resembled humanoid mantises, was extremely fast—even faster than the first wave of Bamboo Pole Ghosts. 
They were clearly ill-suited for sieges, but perfect for pursuit. 
The Bamboo Pole Ghosts, on the other hand, at over three meters tall, were much better suited for storming a city. 
He strongly suspected the Ghost Object outside—the one that had let out that eerie laugh at the beginning—possessed some intelligence. It could probably see the camp's layout and was ready to launch an all-out assault at any moment. 
Though its intelligence probably wasn't very high. Or, at least, the intelligence of the Ghost Objects it controlled wasn't. 
Otherwise, they wouldn't have walked so blindly into the traps. 
"..." 
He looked down at the Exotic Treasure in his hand, his eyes narrowing. It was a piece of Ghost Skin, as thin as a cicada's wing. To the touch, it felt somewhat similar to the Ghost Map. Etched upon the Ghost Skin in crimson lines was a building. 
A structure similar to an Arrow Tower. 
Only it was taller. 
And narrower. 
It was a Building Blueprint, an exceptionally rare type of Exotic Treasure. Among all the Exotic Treasures, Building Blueprints were definitely one of the rarest. This was the first one he had ever acquired. 
- 
[Exotic Treasure Name]: Sentry Tower Building Blueprint 
[Exotic Treasure Level]: White 
[Exotic Treasure Effect]: Allows the construction of a Sentry Tower. The Sentry Tower provides 50 meters of visibility outside the camp.
- 
Without a moment's hesitation, 
He crushed the Building Blueprint in his hand. Unlike other Architects, he didn't need to study it. The moment the Blueprint shattered, a new constructible building, the [Sentry Tower], appeared on the Eternal Night Lord Panel in his mind. It only cost 10 Ghost Stones to build. 
He currently had 130 Ghost Stones. 
The next moment— 
A translucent phantom of an extremely narrow tower, fully ten meters high and composed of countless white lines, materialized in the air. As 10 Ghost Stones in his palm shattered and flowed into it as a milky-white liquid, 
the phantom tower gradually solidified, taking its place beside the Wooden Houses within the walls. 
Its base was merely a meter square. 
It was wider at the bottom and tapered toward the top. 
At its peak was an embedded object resembling a Luminous Pearl. 
The moment the Sentry Tower solidified, his vision instantly expanded. Before, the area outside the camp had been an absolute, soul-devouring darkness, like an abyss. But now, the 50-meter radius beyond the camp's edge had shifted from pitch-black to a faint gray. 
Beyond those 50 meters, the oppressive darkness remained. 
He could clearly see hundreds of Ghost Objects moving in packs through the light gray mist, quickly bypassing his camp and heading deeper into the darkness, like a great animal migration. 
They flashed past in an instant. 
It was hard to see them clearly. 
But... 
Four Bamboo Pole Ghosts were carrying a blood-red palanquin. They hadn't joined the mass migration, but had stopped a steady thirty meters from the camp, surrounded by roughly a hundred other Ghost Objects. 
There were three types in total. 
Bamboo Pole Ghosts and Mantis Ghosts. 
He had named them himself. The third type was the same Monkey Head Ghost he had first encountered. The Monkey Head Ghosts were the fewest in number. 
Among this horde of Ghost Objects, one Bamboo Pole Ghost and one Mantis Ghost were noticeably larger than the others of their kind. 
"..." 
Chen Fan took a deep breath. Standing on the City Wall, he gazed into the gray mist beyond the Ghost Fire. The Sentry Tower had given him a wealth of new information; a fifty-meter range of sight was an enormous help. 
He also finally understood what was happening. 
'In all likelihood...' 
'Those two larger Ghost Objects must be lieutenants. The reason there are so few Monkey Head Ghosts is that I killed so many of them. And there's no lieutenant among them because I already killed it.' 
'After it died, it even dropped two Exotic Treasures for me: the Ghost Ore Vein Seed and the Godslaying Spear.' 
'The one in the palanquin must be the creature that laughed at the start. It found out its subordinate died and came looking for revenge.' 
'Thankfully...' 
A flicker of relief passed through him. The Sentry Tower let him see just how many Ghost Objects were passing by. If that entire horde had charged his camp, he would have been dead without a doubt! 
'There's no way I could have defended against that.' 
Only this one group of Ghost Objects was menacingly waiting outside the camp. The rest simply ignored its existence as they passed, as if they were rushing to get somewhere. 
Two Level 2 Arrow Towers were definitely not going to be enough. 
Back then, the two Level 2 Arrow Towers hadn't killed the Monkey Head Ghost lieutenant on the spot, allowing it to escape the camp and bleed to death dozens of meters away. Now there were two such lieutenants, plus an even stronger main boss. 
The Level 2 Arrow Towers definitely weren't going to cut it. 
'I need a Level 3 Arrow Tower.' 
Fortunately, he had saved enough Ghost Stones to upgrade the Arrow Towers. 
Chapter 25: Arrow Tower Upgraded to Level 3 
Upgrading a Level 2 Arrow Tower to a Level 3 Arrow Tower required 50 Ghost Stones. 
He currently had 120 Ghost Stones. 
He didn't hesitate. 
He took out five glowing green Level 2 Ghost Stones from his robes. Immediately, the Ghost Stones shattered, transforming into wisps of eerie green liquid that floated in the air and flew toward the nearest Level 2 Arrow Tower. 
A panel quickly popped up before him. 
- 
[Arrow Tower upgraded to Level 3.] 
[Please select an Upgrade Direction.] 
[1: Crossbow Arrows will be imbued with the Fire Element, increasing their power.] 
[2: Unlocks the Active Skill 'Arrow Rain Storm.' When activated, the Crossbow Mechanism on top of the tower will rapidly fire upward, unleashing a dense volley of arrows upon a designated area for several seconds. Costs 1 Ghost Stone.] 
[3: Crossbow Arrows will be imbued with the Thunder Element, effectively paralyzing enemies and dealing chain damage to up to three nearby creatures upon impact.] 
- 
'An Active Skill.' 
Chen Fan's eyes narrowed slightly as he looked at the Upgrade Directions. This was the first time he had encountered an Active Skill as an upgrade option, and it looked very effective. 
'If we encounter a swarm of Ghost Objects charging the camp...' 
'...one skill could take care of them.' 
'And the cost is low, only one Ghost Stone.'
After the Arrow Tower was upgraded to Level 3, it began to slowly transform. First, its original wood-and-stone frame became pure stone, seamlessly integrating with the City Wall. 
It had become much sturdier. 
The power of the Crossbow Arrows from the Active Skill named "Arrow Rain Storm" was equivalent to that of a Level 2 Arrow Tower. It wasn't a significant power boost, but it would be extremely useful against a Ghost Tide with large numbers of weak enemies. 
Although he was tempted... 
...in the end, he still chose the first Upgrade Direction, once again boosting the tower's single-target damage.
The two minor Ghost Object bosses outside the camp, along with the female ghost of unknown strength in the sedan chair, were still eyeing him menacingly. Right now, what he needed most was a way to threaten those three. 
The moment he made his choice... 
...the Arrow Tower transformed again. 
The Crossbow Mechanism at the top slowly changed from its original pitch-black to a dark red. The nocks at the front of the mechanism burst into roaring flames that danced defiantly in the downpour. The entire Crossbow Mechanism became more intimidating, more soul-shaking.
'From the looks of it...' 
'...it seems that for every level a building gains, three Upgrade Directions will pop up.' 
'And these three Upgrade Directions seem to be fixed.' 
'At least, the options for both of his Arrow Towers were identical.' 
Just then, Lame Monkey's urgent voice suddenly rang in his ears. "Young Master, a Ghost Tide is charging us!" 
Chen Fan followed Lame Monkey's gaze. Dozens of meters outside the camp, a huge swarm of Ghost Objects surged from the north like a tide, heading straight for the camp with no intention of turning aside. 
His brow furrowed slightly. 
This group of Ghost Objects didn't belong to the three bosses. They were a traveling batch of Ghost Objects from the Eternal Night. They were small, about the size of a human child, and had no limbs. Their entire bodies were plump balls of flesh, and the surface of the flesh was covered with human faces about the size of a palm. 
It was an extremely bizarre sight. 
They moved by rolling. 
There were many of them—well over a hundred at a glance—and a continuous stream of more fleshy balls was emerging from the gray mist behind them, with no end in sight. 
Their speed, however, was not fast; they ambled along as if they were out for a stroll. 
One could even discern emotions on the multiple faces covering the fleshy balls. At first, their faces all wore joyful smiles, but as they neared the camp, every face on the balls turned ugly, contorting into expressions of great pain. 
"Station Chief, those are Meat Worm Ghosts." 
Zhou Mo, standing on the City Wall with his Big Saber in hand, spoke in a rapid, urgent tone. "I know these Ghost Objects. They only appear during the rainy season and are never seen in the normal Eternal Night. They have no attacks—their only method of attack is self-destruction." 
"They explode upon contact with any relatively hard object, their entire bodies turning into a viscous green liquid. It's highly corrosive. If a human is touched by this liquid, they will melt in extreme agony." 
"They can cause considerable damage to the City Wall."
"The Meat Worm Ghost is the culprit behind the fall of many cities during the rainy season. These Ghost Objects are social creatures that surge forward like a tide. Once they appear in swarms, it often means the fall of the city walls!" 
"..." 
Chen Fan didn't reply. Time was of the essence. Even though the Meat Worm Ghosts weren't fast, they were now only a dozen or so meters from the camp. Some were even briefly lifted into the air by the gale-force winds before falling back to the ground and continuing to roll forward. 
He glanced toward the sedan chair in the gray mist to the west of the camp. 
The moment this swarm of Meat Worm Ghosts appeared, the sedan chair and its surrounding Ghost Objects had withdrawn from the gray mist and vanished into the Eternal Night. 
'How considerate!' 
He couldn't help but take a deep breath. He once again pulled five glowing green Ghost Stones from his robes, slapping them onto the adjacent Level 2 Arrow Tower. As he upgraded it to Level 3, he chose the "Arrow Rain Storm" Active Skill Upgrade Direction!
The camp was now at a moment of life and death. 
If both groups of Ghost Objects had attacked the camp together, it would have been extremely tricky. Fortunately, the female ghost had withdrawn her underlings into the Eternal Night. Of course, he didn't think she was genuinely unwilling to use superior numbers to overwhelm him. 
It was obvious that she also feared the Meat Worm Ghosts and didn't want to get caught in the crossfire. 
"YAAA!!!" 
An extremely piercing shriek echoed through the camp. The first Meat Worm Ghost had broken through the gray mist and charged into the camp. Upon sensing the presence of the Ghost Fire, the many faces on the fleshy ball uncontrollably let out horrific screams, their expressions twisting in extreme pain. 
They clearly did not want to get near the Ghost Fire. 
But their bodies obediently continued to roll straight toward the camp. 
As more and more Meat Worm Ghosts rushed into the camp, a cacophony of overlapping, piercing screams filled the air. 
The next moment— 
The runes inlaid on the Crossbow Mechanism atop a Level 3 Arrow Tower on the City Wall suddenly flared brightly. Dozens of complex, arcane runes glowed with white light, lifting off the mechanism to float around the tower. 
Then, with an almost inaudible click of a mechanism...

Then, like divine punishment, they rained down, intersecting with the torrential rain to completely cover an area north of the City Wall. 
"TSSSSS..." 
Dozens of rolling Meat Worm Ghosts were caught in the disaster and almost completely annihilated. A sizzling sound, like red-hot iron pokers plunged into butter, echoed out. The light arrows slowly dissipated, leaving behind only the remains of the Meat Worm Ghosts, which dissolved into pools of green liquid that corroded the wasteland. 
One after another, Ghost Stones dropped from their bodies, lying quietly in the middle of the liquid, looking extremely tempting. 
The first wave of the Meat Worm Ghosts' charge was almost completely wiped out. 
The area of effect of the arrow rain wasn't that large, so a few slipped through the net. 
Although a few of these stragglers charged into the camp, their path wasn't toward the City Wall. Instead, they skirted the edge of the wall, quickly passed through the camp, and continued rolling on into the Eternal Night, without a hint of looking back or lingering.
